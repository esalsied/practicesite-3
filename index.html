Personal Narrative

When I first came to George Mason, I wanted to pursue a career in the healthcare industry. More specifically, I was conflicted between becoming a psychologist and becoming a physician, leaning more towards becoming a psychologist. This is indicated both by the classes I took during my first three semesters at George Mason as well as my having worked as a CNA during my freshman year.

During the Fall semester of my sophomore year, I realized that I no longer wanted to pursue psychology and chose to shift my focus towards English and writing in general. English and writing have always come more naturally to me than other disciplines so I figured that it would make sense to center my education around them. Although I still have some catching up to do within the major, I feel more satisfied majoring in English than I was majoring in psychology.

Currently, I am pursuing a career as a technical writer although I am definitely open to exploring other career paths. Despite my past experience with coursework in the hard and social sciences, I would like to focus my technical writing career on technology-related fields like software, web design, etc. This was largely motivated by the flexibility that technology-related technical writing offers; something I value in a career.

Another career path I have considered is becoming an editor mainly because, in the past, I have found that editing is something that generally comes naturally to me especially when it comes to reading writing in an essay format. While distinct from being an editor, I hope that being part of the Writing Center here at George Mason will give me a better sense of what I would like to do careerwise.
